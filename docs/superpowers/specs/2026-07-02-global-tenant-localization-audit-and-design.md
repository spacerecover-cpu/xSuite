# Global Tenant Localization — Audit & Architecture Design

**Date:** 2026-07-02
**Status:** PROPOSED — no implementation yet
**Audience:** platform owner + future implementing engineers (assumes zero prior context)
**Scope:** everything a tenant's country touches — tax computation and filing, currency and money precision, country configuration, statutory documents, dates/fiscal calendars, document numbering, accounting/reporting, i18n and addresses, payroll/HR, privacy/residency, fiscalization regimes, platform subscription billing, late-payment law, fixed assets, and units of measure.

This document has three parts. **Part 1** is the verified codebase audit: 289 finding rows organized into 16 dimensions, every row carrying a file:line citation, each verified against the repo and the live canonical database (`ssmbegiyjivrcwgcqutu`). **Part 2** is the recommended architecture — the Statutory Plugin Kernel (SPK+) — selected by a scored three-way architecture panel and upgraded with twelve grafts from the losing proposals. **Part 3** walks Oman, India, and the US through the final design end to end. Appendices carry the live-DB ground truth, the domain invariants the design must not break, the panel scorecard, the alternatives honestly summarized, and open questions for the owner.

### 1.2 Tax engine

The tax engine is a single-flat-rate, tax-exclusive VAT machine bolted onto a genuinely good country-config chassis. The country engine resolves tax system/label/default-rate/number-format per country fail-loud, forms default from it, PDFs get a country override layer, and a DB-trigger-driven `vat_records` loop gives GCC-style tenants a real (if minimal) compliance loop. But the calculation core is one scalar `tax_rate` applied document-wide, so India's CGST/SGST/IGST split and US jurisdiction stacking are unrepresentable; two ledger-integrity bugs produce wrong filed numbers in any country (credit-note reversals written to an unread table; no currency/base dimension on the ledger); and there is no inclusive pricing, no tax-treatment classification, no effective-dated rates, no buyer identity on documents, and a GCC-only 3-number return shape. The blueprint for most fixes already exists in `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-15-country-engine-design.md`.

### 1.3 Currency & money

Currency-money is the strongest country dimension in xSuite: multicurrency Phase 1 is fully live (frozen per-document rates, `*_base` snapshot columns, base-currency stats RPCs, realized FX in `record_payment`, an actively-refreshing rate feed, currency-aware rounding, and a fail-loud no-USD-fabrication config engine). The design must build on `financialMath`/`currencyService.resolveRateContext` and the `*_base` invariant, not reinvent them. The breakage clusters in four places: modules the base-currency model never reached (VAT ledger, bank transfers, payroll/WPS, the `numeric(12,2)` columns M3 didn't widen), the `numeric(5,2)` tax-rate columns, reference-data coverage (9 onboardable countries, 35 currencies), and read-side display drift. One claim in this dimension was refuted during verification (the refuted-claims count of 1 belongs here).

### 1.4 Country configuration

The country-config framework is architecturally strong — a registry-driven, Zod-validated, fail-loud cascade (geo bag → tenant snapshot → tenant overrides) with governed overrides, stub-country provisioning gates, and a PDF country layer — but the pipeline leaks at both ends. At the entry end, self-service signup is broken for all countries right now, and the provisioning sync trigger is provably dead for every column carrying a NOT NULL US default. In the middle, the DB snapshot mapper writes only 13 of the registry's keys. At the model level, tax is single-component/single-rate and e-invoicing is one hardcoded `'SA'` branch. Null/absent config correctly fails loud (no silent US fallback), and tenant overrides are properly governed — the spine is right; the fixes are the trigger/default collision, snapshot key coverage, and the single-component tax model.

### 1.5 Documents & compliance

### 1.10 Hardcoded-value sweep

The country engine is real and recent — layered config with fail-loud sentinels, multicurrency base threading, per-currency minor units, a PDF country layer, fiscal-ready numbering schema. ### 1.11 Payroll & HR

Payroll/HR is informal-everywhere, with a distinctly Omani accent. The engine computes pay as basic salary + regular-rate overtime − flat 7% "social security" − loan installments. There is no income-tax/withholding engine of any kind, the generic salary-component model is never read by the engine, payslips can't itemize (their source table is never written), unpaid leave never reduces pay, and the "bank file" is a pipe-delimited text no bank accepts. ## Part 2 — Recommended Architecture & Implementation Design

### Recommended Global Architecture

**The decision: SPK+ — the Statutory Plugin Kernel with Governed Data Publishing.** The final recommended architecture is the Statutory Plugin Kernel (SPK) — typed per-regime strategy plugins over a shared fiscal kernel, with the DB sealing regime-blind invariants at one issuance choke point — upgraded with all twelve judge grafts from the competing proposals (Appendix C). The result keeps SPK's winning posture on the three existential criteria (provably-correct money, live-tenant migration with untouched history, forensic provenance) while adopting Config-First's publish governance and the Hybrid's kernel-primitive cost curve and migration hygiene.

**The one-sentence contract:** statutory *algorithms* (place-of-supply splits, jurisdiction stacking, inclusive back-out, return composition, clearance protocols) are typed TypeScript plugins proven by externally-validated golden fixtures gated in CI **and replayed from the database at every pack publish**; statutory *facts* (rates, requirements, rounding policy, notations, numbering formats, regime bindings) are effective-dated, pack-versioned, dual-control-published data; and Postgres seals what it can verify regime-blind — atomic numbering, sum-consistency, data-declared field-requirement gating inside the issue RPC, append-only base-currency tax ledger, custody events, post-issuance immutability — so no PostgREST client can bypass compliance.

**Grafts absorbed (all 12):** (1) DB-resident golden fixtures (`master_country_pack_tests`) machine-replayed at publish time, closing the "admin edits a rate row, repo CI never fires" wrong-money hole; (2) dual-control publishing + a machine-checked `master_engine_capabilities` manifest — `statutory_ready` is machine-derived, never hand-asserted, with per-pack staleness/next-review metadata; (3) the design-doc canonical table name **`geo_country_tax_rates`** (§3c) instead of SPK's `geo_tax_rules` rename; (4) rounding policy and amount-in-words scale system as pack **data** consumed by strategies (strategies supply only defaults); (5) a persisted computation trace / explain mode on previews and issued documents; (6) backfilled tax lines rendered distinctly everywhere and excluded from filings for pre-activation periods; (7) the three currency layers (document / base / jurisdiction) named explicitly, with the per-registration-base seam reserved; (8) `in_gst` and `us_sales_tax` implemented as **parameterizations of shared kernel primitives** (split-by-place-of-supply, jurisdiction-stack), capping long-run per-country code; (9) largest-remainder allocation as the single sanctioned distribution primitive in `financialMath`; (10) NOT VALID→VALIDATE integrity enforcement plus standing NULL-base/NULL-rate monitors; (11) declarative field-requirement rows evaluated **inside** the issue RPC (REST-unskippable); (12) publish→resync discipline with pack pinning, snapshot-immunity, staged rollout — and an immediate block on cross-currency bank transfers.

**Judge criticisms resolved:** SPK's honest weakness — complex regimes need a code release — is narrowed three ways: shared defaults (`simple_vat`/`prefix_numbering`/`generic_invoice`/`no_einvoice`) keep ~80% of countries data-only; kernel-primitive parameterization means Canada/Australia/GCC-variant regimes reuse existing machinery with new data plus a thin parameter object; and the authoring/publish surface makes every rate change, requirement change, and rounding change a governed data edit. The residual — a genuinely novel statutory algorithm still costs an engineering sprint — is retained deliberately: the audit proves that config-only pretending ships silent non-compliance.

**What ships value first:** Phase 0 (1–2 weeks) fixes every live wrong-number today — broken signup (`geoCountryService.ts:40`), USD-default tenant provisioning (`sync_tenant_config_from_country` defaults-before-trigger), credit-note VAT vanishing into unread `vat_transactions`, UTC period boundaries, the ×100 rate display, precision truncation of OMR mils, the `*_base` backfill (all 993 invoices currently NULL), and the cross-currency transfer guard. Phase 1 proves Oman byte-identical through the new engine over the full 993-invoice corpus. India flips `statutory_ready` in Phase 4, US in Phase 5. The migration window is uniquely cheap **now**: the active production tenant base is Omani-only, the VAT ledger is empty, and the base columns are NULL — this program must run before more tenants land.

#### Six layers plus a governance plane

**The boundary rule (stated once, enforced everywhere):** code may know *how* to compute tax, render a document, compose a return, or mint a number; only governed data may say *what* a given country requires — except where the "what" is itself an algorithm (a split rule, a stack rule, a clearance protocol), in which case it is a typed, versioned, fixture-proven plugin selected *by* data. No `if (countryCode === ...)` may exist outside `src/lib/regimes/` (eslint rule + CI grep, landed Phase 1 — the institutionalized lesson of `einvoiceRouting.ts:6` and `invoiceAdapter.ts:38`).

**Why computation runs in TS and sealing runs in Postgres:** place-of-supply logic, nexus tables, provider seams (Avalara), and 30+ future regimes are unmaintainable and untestable in plpgsql — vitest golden fixtures per country are the compliance evidence. But JS-only checks are bypassable via PostgREST (a verified critical). So the DB owns everything it can verify **without regime knowledge**: arithmetic consistency, atomic numbering, requirement rows evaluated as data, append-only ledgers, immutability. The DB accepts only internally-consistent documents; the plugin guarantees legal consistency, proven by fixtures replayed in two places — repo CI on every commit, and `master_country_pack_tests` at every data publish (graft 1). This split is the same posture already shipped in `record_payment`, `post_invoice_vat_record`, and `prevent_audit_mutation`.

**Forensic provenance (the DR-lab brand requirement):** every `document_tax_lines` row stamps `regime_key + plugin_version + pack_version_id + rule_trace` (the explain trace, graft 5); document headers carry a frozen `regime_snapshot`; e-invoice artifacts are sealed append-only with `payload_hash`/`previous_hash`. "Which code and which published data produced this statutory figure" is always answerable from the row alone.

#### Calculation algorithm (one path for quote, invoice, credit note, POS sale)

1. **Fact assembly (TS):** `TaxContext` = seller (legal entity + active `legal_entity_tax_registrations`), buyer snapshot (tax_number, structured-address `subdivision_id`, country), `taxPointDate = tenantToday(timezone)` or explicit `supply_date` (fixes UTC 'today' stamping at `invoiceService.ts:829`), lines, doc discount, `tax_inclusive`, `rateContext` from `currencyService.resolveRateContext` (the existing seam, reused).
2. **Regime resolution:** `regime.tax` via the Country Engine cascade keyed on the **resolving legal entity's** `country_id` (kills `normalizeSaudi`, `invoiceAdapter.ts:38`). Unregistered key on a `statutory_ready` country → `CountryConfigError`, render blocked — structurally impossible to print silent "VAT 0%".
3. **Pack-data resolution:** rates effective-dated from `geo_country_tax_rates` at `taxPointDate`; **rounding policy `{mode, level, cash_increment}` and words scale-system read as pack data**, strategy supplies only defaults (graft 4) — a future half_even or CHF-0.05 country is a data edit.
4. **Document-discount allocation:** largest-remainder across lines *before* tax (graft 9) — Σ(line taxable) ≡ document taxable by construction, killing the `invoiceService.ts:483-487` vs `financialMath.ts:60-61` header/line drift. Example (OMR 3dp): lines 100.000×3, discount 0.100 → exact shares 0.03333… → floor 0.033 each = 0.099; remainder 0.001 assigned by largest fractional remainder (stable order) → allocations 0.034/0.033/0.033; Σ = 0.100 exactly.
5. **Classification per line:** treatment ∈ standard / reduced / zero_rated(+reason) / exempt(+reason) / **reverse_charge** (0-amount components on the document, mandatory notation emitted, subledger rows flagged for box mapping) / **out_of_scope** (US no-nexus, statutory interest lines). Fixes the "every sale is standard-rated" gap (`database.types.ts:12447`).
6. **Component set (kernel scheme modes — graft 8):** `single` → [country component]; `split_by_place_of_supply` → seller registration subdivision vs `place_of_supply`: intra-state → CGST+SGST pair (two rate rows), inter-state → IGST; `jurisdiction_stack` → walk the buyer ship-to subdivision path, include each `geo_country_tax_rates` row where a `legal_entity_tax_registrations` row exists for the state (nexus as data), else out_of_scope.
7. **Inclusive back-out** where `tax_inclusive`: base = gross × 100/(100+Σrates), then largest-remainder so the agreed gross is preserved *exactly*. Example: ₹5,000 inclusive @18% split CGST/SGST → base 4,237.29; total tax must be 762.71 (5,000 − 4,237.29); naive halves are 381.355 each → 381.36 + 381.36 = 762.72 (off by 0.01); largest-remainder yields 381.36 + 381.35 = 762.71 and gross reconstitutes to 5,000.00 exactly.
8. **Rounding:** all amounts through `roundMoneyWith(policy)` at **document-currency** decimals (0/2/3-dp safe: 1,250.000 × 5% = 62.500 OMR keeps the mil). `level:'line'` regimes (KSA, India) round components per line then sum; the header is *definitionally* the sum — never independently recomputed (fixes the PDF re-derive finding at `invoiceAdapter.ts:146`). `cash_increment` (Indian whole-rupee s.170) emits an explicit out-of-scope rounding adjustment line, visible on the document, never smeared into tax.
9. **Totals + base:** header tax = Σ rollups; totals in document currency first (the legal PDF figures); each component row's `tax_amount_base` computed once at the frozen rate (multicurrency §3.3 invariant).
10. **Trace (graft 5):** every step appends to a deterministic trace — matched rate-row ids, scheme decision, allocation remainders, rounding ops. Returned on dry-runs; persisted into `rule_trace` at issuance. Support can answer "why does this invoice differ by a baisa from last month's" from the row.
11. **Issuance — `issue_tax_document(p_doc_type, p_doc_id)` SECURITY DEFINER, one transaction:** (a) FOR UPDATE lock; (b) evaluate `master_document_requirements` rows (`{field, condition, level: block|warn, message}`) **in-RPC** (graft 11) — an Indian B2B invoice without buyer GSTIN/place-of-supply/HSN cannot be issued, and no raw REST writer can skip the gate; (c) mint the number in-transaction via `get_next_number` v2 (closes the pre-mint gap at `invoiceService.ts:411`); (d) validate Σ(`document_tax_lines`) = header tax and `_base` variants to currency-decimal tolerance; (e) post `vat_records` per component in base currency with `tax_period` = tenant-local YYYY-MM of the tax point (extends the proven expense-side pattern; fixes last-day-of-period exclusion at `vatService.ts:116`); (f) `EInvoicingTransport` hook seals the statutory artifact into `einvoice_submissions`; (g) write the `chain_of_custody` 'financial' event (v1.2.0 invariant preserved); (h) flip to issued — the immutability trigger then rejects mutation outside the whitelist (the NF525/GoBD storage-level lock; fixes `invoiceService.ts:686`).
12. **Withholding (India TDS / KSA WHT):** the strategy stamps `expected_withholding`; `record_payment` gains `withheld_amount + certificate_ref`, settles the receivable in full, posts a tax-credit ledger row.
13. **Credit/debit notes:** same engine, sign flipped, own numbering scope; contra rows post to `vat_records` — the file-and-overpay bug is structurally gone.

#### Audit trail of every computed figure

Every persisted component row answers: which plugin (`regime_key`+`plugin_version`), which published data (`pack_version_id`), which rate rows and decisions (`rule_trace`), which treatment and why (`treatment_reason_code`), and whether it is engine-computed truth or reconstruction (`backfilled` — rendered distinctly on every audit surface and **excluded from filings** for pre-activation periods, graft 6). #### Versioning and effective dating (two mechanisms, deliberately separate)

- **Rates** are effective-dated **rows**: a Bahrain-style 5→10 change is one new `valid_from` row in a new pack version — no deploy, no reseed, no tenant resync; past documents untouched because they carry frozen tax lines; future documents resolve by their own tax-point date.

### Financial Calculation Engine

#### Money representation

- All money columns `numeric(19,4)` — this program completes the M3 sweep into the stragglers the audit verified at `numeric(12,2)`: `vat_records.vat_amount`, `vat_returns.output/input/net_vat`, `account_transfers.amount`, `payroll_records.net_salary`, `stock_*`, `payment_receipts`/`payment_disbursements`, `purchase_orders`, and — critically — `invoice_line_items.unit_price`/`quote_items.unit_price`, which today cannot store a 3-dp OMR unit price.
- All tax rates `numeric(7,4)` platform-wide (widened from `numeric(5,2)`): NYC 8.875 and county x.x75 rates storable; fixes the rate-corruption blocker in `supabase/migrations.manifest.md:51`.
- FX rates `numeric(20,10)` (also normalizing `receipts.exchange_rate` from its inconsistent `numeric(10,6)`).
- `master_currency_codes` seeded to full ISO-4217 (35 → all); `getCurrencyDecimals` **throws** on unknown codes (`currencyService.ts:23`) — fail-loud per the platform's D2 posture, never a silent 2-dp assumption.

#### The three currency layers (graft 7 — named once, never conflated)

1. **DOCUMENT currency** — the legal figures on the PDF. All tax is computed and rounded here first, at this currency's decimals.
2. **BASE currency** — `tenants.base_currency_code` functional currency. `*_base = round(amount × frozen rate, base dp)` written **once** at write time through `currencyService.resolveRateContext` — the single seam; every new writer (including `document_tax_lines`) MUST use it, never re-derive.
3. **JURISDICTION currency** — the filing currency of the resolving legal entity's country. Today, single-entity tenants have base == jurisdiction; the ReturnComposer **asserts this loudly** at composition time (`ConfigError`, never a silent mixed sum). The upgrade seam is documented and reserved — per-registration base columns on the tax ledger (`tax_amount_reg_base` + `reg_exchange_rate` per `legal_entity_tax_registrations`) for future multi-jurisdiction entities — so it never gets invented ad hoc.

This is what makes an Oman lab invoicing in EUR file a correct OMR return: EUR VAT converts at the document's frozen rate at write time (satisfying GCC/UK prescribed-rate rules), and the ledger/returns sum only base — fixing the mixed-currency filing critical (`vat_records` at `database.types.ts:16652`, `vatService.ts:124`).

#### Rounding policy

One implementation: `financialMath.roundMoneyWith(policy)` where `policy = {mode: 'half_up'|'half_even', level: 'line'|'document', cash_increment?: number}` comes from **pack data** (graft 4); strategies supply only defaults. `simple_vat` keeps today's half-up document-level behavior so the Oman tenant is byte-identical (the parity requirement). Per-line regimes round components per line then sum; the header is definitionally the sum. Cash/invoice rounding (Indian whole-rupee, future CHF 0.05) emits an explicit out-of-scope adjustment line.

#### Allocation discipline (graft 9)

**Largest-remainder allocation is the only sanctioned way to split any document-level amount** across lines or components — document discounts, inclusive back-outs, credit-note proration. It guarantees Σ(parts) == whole at the target precision, which is what makes HSN summaries and ZATCA line XML reconcile with headers **by construction**. An eslint rule (sibling of the existing `xsuite/no-raw-currency-aggregation`) bans ad-hoc proportional splits.

#### FX composition (Phase 1 multicurrency composes, never re-derives)

Decisions A/B/C stand intact: frozen `exchange_rate` + `rate_source` + `*_base` snapshotted at write; USD-pivot shared `exchange_rates` with carry-forward gap policy; manual overrides row-local; realized FX at settlement inside `record_payment`. ### Testing Strategy

#### 1. Golden compliance fixtures (the legal evidence — dual-resident by design)

Each regime plugin ships `fixtures/`: canonical documents with **externally-validated** expected component splits, totals, notations, numbering shapes, and return boxes. Fixtures are the compliance evidence *and* the regression net, resident in two places running the same runner (`src/lib/tax/publishGate.ts`): **repo CI** (`statutory-fixtures` job — enumerates `statutory_ready` countries from the live DB, asserts every regime key resolves to a registered plugin, runs its fixtures; a country cannot be statutory_ready with missing/failing fixtures) and **`master_country_pack_tests`** replayed at every data publish (graft 1 — fires when a platform admin edits one rate row, which repo CI never sees).

#### 2. Multi-country document matrix

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **OM** | VAT 5% single row, 3dp, bilingual | TAX INVOICE, VATIN band, baisa words | contra ledger row nets Q return | GCC 3-box quarterly, Asia/Muscat boundaries |

| **US** | 3-component stack; out-of-scope no-nexus; exempt-state | 'Invoice', Letter, no ceremony, MM/DD | jurisdiction-split reversal | per-jurisdiction remittance |
| **UK** | 20/5/0 mixed-rate document | buyer VAT no., reverse-charge notation | contra | MTD 9-box, stagger-group boundaries |

Every cell asserts: computed component rows, header=Σ invariant, `*_base` values, rendered PDF snapshot, React preview parity, and the persisted `rule_trace` (deterministic — same inputs, byte-identical trace).

#### 3. Property-based tests (rounding and allocation)

- **Largest-remainder totality:** ∀ random amounts × decimals ∈ {0,2,3} × n parts: Σ(parts) ≡ whole exactly; parts differ pairwise by ≤ one minor unit; deterministic under stable ordering.
- **Inclusive round-trip:** ∀ gross, rates: base + Σ(components) reconstitutes gross exactly at document decimals.
- **Rounding modes:** half_up vs half_even boundary values at 0/2/3dp; `cash_increment` adjustment line always out_of_scope and exactly closes the gap.
- **Base invariant:** `*_base = round(amount × rate, base dp)` for every persisted row (mirrors the multicurrency §3.3 SQL/TS parity precedent).

#### 4. Migration rehearsal and parity

Full rehearsal on a **Supabase branch** (`mcp__supabase__create_branch`) against a production snapshot: M-A→M-J executed in order; the M-E gate replays **all 993 invoices + 1,138 quotes** and diffs totals/tax to zero; backfill assertions (row counts, SUM parity, `backfilled=true` counts); NOT VALID→VALIDATE succeeds; rollback drill (flags off, legacy path resumes).

#### 5. Security, RLS, and bypass tests

Cross-tenant read/write attempts on every new table (RESTRICTIVE isolation verified); anon-key probes of every new/changed RPC (SEC-1 grant posture); **PostgREST bypass suite:** direct REST attempts to (a) insert an internally-inconsistent invoice (integrity trigger rejects), (b) mutate an issued invoice/tax line (immutability trigger rejects), (c) issue an India B2B invoice missing GSTIN via raw RPC (requirement gate rejects), (d) write `vat_transactions` (REVOKE), (e) rewind a legal-scope sequence as non-admin.

#### 6. Invariant regression suites

Custody 'financial' events asserted across the write-path flip (v1.2.0); append-only behavior of `einvoice_submissions`/`vat_records` contra discipline; shadow-mode divergence report must be zero for one release before legacy retirement; one shadow return period hand-reconciled against current vatService math. Standing production monitors (NULL base/rates, overdue pack reviews) are themselves tested with seeded violations.

#### 7. i18n and display

Per-language snapshot tests (en byte-identical — the Phase-4a invariant); lakh-grouping and indian-scale-words fixtures; RTL bilingual PDF snapshots; portal ar-coverage parity test extended; date-format matrix (DD/MM vs MM/DD surfaces) across the six countries.

### A.2 Live gaps the design must close

- **OMR 3-decimal precision loss at line level:** `unit_price`/`discount` `numeric(12,2)` on line items, and all VAT amounts `numeric(12,2)` — while the live tenant IS an OMR/3dp tenant.
- **Weekend facts wrong for the Gulf in the relational column** ([0,6] for all 58 incl. Oman/Saudi); the correct values exist only in nested jsonb nothing reads; tenants have NO weekend/week-start/time-format/separator columns at all.
- **The sync trigger can never sync NOT-NULL-defaulted columns** — country sync works only if provisioning explicitly inserts NULLs, a fragile contract the schema cannot enforce, and it never re-syncs on country change.
- **Fiscal-year numbering is schema-only** — the columns exist (all NULL) and `get_next_number` ignores them; `fiscal_year_start` ('04-01', '04-06') is never consumed by numbering.
- **The VAT/GST compliance layer is empty and structurally thin** — 0 rows across all four tax tables despite a live VAT tenant with 993 invoices; single scalar rates; no component split, place-of-supply, HSN/SAC, reverse charge, or multiple registrations.
- **Base-currency reporting not materialized** — all `*_base` NULL; `exchange_rates` is USD-pivot only with history starting 2026-05-30, so older documents have no attestable rate.
- **`convert_proforma_to_tax_invoice` drops line-level tax detail** and hardcodes a 'USD' currency fallback.
- **42 of 58 countries are unusable stubs** that would silently inherit US-dollar defaults if activated naively; `statutory_workweek` and `data_protection_regime` are NULL for all 58.
- **Payroll config contradictions with no country awareness** — two sources of truth; globally seeded 'Income Tax' is wrong for Oman/UAE; components carry no computable semantics.
- **Inconsistent numeric precision across money columns** — exchange_rate at three different precisions; line items scale 2 vs headers scale 4; no DB-enforced money convention.
- **Sequence scopes inconsistent and padding outgrown** — magic strings; unknown scopes silently mint new sequences; per-device-type scopes embed raw UUIDs.

## Appendix B — Domain constraints & invariants

The 16-stage data-recovery lifecycle, the custody model, and the shipped financial machinery impose constraints this design must satisfy. Verified facts first, then domain gaps the localization program touches but does not own.

### B.1 Facts and invariants

- **Stage 7 (Quotation) reality.** Two parallel quote systems exist: staff write `quotes`/`quote_items` (multicurrency base snapshotting real), while the portal READS `case_quotes` (0 rows, never written). `approve_quote`/`reject_quote` look up status names ('Approved'/'Rejected') that don't exist in `master_quote_statuses` ('Accepted'/'Declined'). Quote approval is not a documented authorization-to-attempt and doesn't advance the case. A localization program touching quote statuses/labels must align with this known defect, not localize the broken vocabulary.
- **Stage 14 (Billing) reality.** `invoices`/`invoice_line_items`/`payments`/`payment_allocations`/`financial_transactions`; proforma→tax-invoice via RPC; invoice status is DERIVED, not a state machine; `recordPayment` enforces tax-invoice-only. Domain rule: "release of data is the leverage point — no full payment, no data"; evaluation-fee/recovery-fee/no-data-no-fee pricing is the norm in real labs.
- **Delivery gates (stages 12/13/15) touch money.** No recovered-file manifest and no customer delivery-acceptance gate exist anywhere; checkout (`log_case_checkout`) has no permission/status/payment gate and force-sets `cases.status='Delivered'` via raw SECURITY DEFINER UPDATE; transition `requires[]` preconditions are advisory-only — a case can close fully unpaid with no QA. (Cataloged gaps: backlog #3 payment-before-release, backlog #9 broken approval loop; re-verify current gate status on main before relying on the doc's snapshot.)
- **Financial events write chain of custody (v1.2.0).** Quote/invoice/payment events write `chain_of_custody` 'financial' entries from the financial services; device intake writes DEVICE_RECEIVED via DB trigger. Any refactor of invoiceService/quotesService/paymentsService MUST preserve these custody writes.
- **Document/PDF surfaces per stage.** pdfmake is the sole PDF library; case receipt/label at Stage 2/4, quote/invoice PDFs at 7/14 (document-currency amounts are the legally-correct figures), checkout form with physical signature at 13, bilingual reports at 16. PDFs intentionally do not theme. Note: Document Studio shipped after the workflow doc (PR #340) and a Typst renderer exists — re-verify document plumbing against main when implementing.
- **Stage 16 reality (post-doc corrections).** `financial_audit_logs` is frontend-dormant and `audit_trails` written only by user-management services; BUT the VAT ledger went live on 2026-06-10 (trigger posting `vat_records` on tax-invoice issuance + append-only contra rows on void, 11 historical invoices backfilled) and purchase-side input VAT with idempotency landed 2026-06-15 — the workflow doc's "dormant VAT" snapshot is outdated on this point.
- **Receipts/online payment.** Atomic `create_receipt_with_allocations` exists and is deliberately base-currency-only (foreign-currency invoices rejected → payments path); receipts gained FX columns; online payment capture for case invoices remains absent (PayPal functions are subscription-only).
- **Financial RLS hardened since the workflow doc.** 318 always-true write policies were rewritten (2026-06-09): financial money/banking/VAT tables gated `has_role('accounts')`, HR→`has_role('hr')`, rest→`is_staff_user()`. Localization migrations touching financial tables must preserve these role gates plus RESTRICTIVE tenant isolation.
- **Atomic money RPCs replaced JS allocation.** `record_payment`/`void_payment` are single-transaction SECURITY DEFINER RPCs (FOR UPDATE, Σ(allocations)=amount, same-currency invariant payment.currency=invoice.currency — mixed-currency deferred to Phase 2 FX); void posts a reversing negative ledger row. The old non-atomic JS path was removed.
- **Invariant — custody & append-only audit tables.** Nine audit tables have REVOKEd UPDATE/DELETE + `prevent_audit_mutation()`; custody transfers have exactly one governed exception RPC (pending→accepted/rejected). Do NOT represent custody as court-grade tamper-evidence: no hash chaining, `evidence_hash` never written, `entry_number` row-order-derived. Never weaken any of this.
- **Invariant — tenancy, soft deletes, migration discipline.** Every tenant table: `tenant_id NOT NULL` + RESTRICTIVE isolation + tenant/audit trigger + partial index (CI-asserted). Soft deletes only; additive migrations only; every schema change through `apply_migration` → regenerate types → update callers → manifest row → migration PR template; CI gates: typecheck=0, schema-drift, migration-manifest, tenant-table-requirements, banned legacy names.
- **Invariant — the frozen-rate money model (keystone).** Every financial document stores transaction currency + frozen `exchange_rate numeric(20,10)` + `rate_source` + `*_base` computed ONCE at write (`*_base = round(amount × rate, base decimals)`). Never convert history on read; edits reuse the frozen rate; receivables carried at booked rate with FX captured separately; document totals computed in the DOCUMENT currency first and never derived from base; single-currency tenants byte-identical (rate 1, base==raw).
- **Invariant — no hardcoded currency/tax/date.** Use TenantConfigContext (`useCurrencyConfig`/`useTaxConfig`, `formatCurrencyWithConfig`); TaxSystem ∈ VAT|GST|SALES_TAX|NONE; per-currency `decimal_places` lives on `master_currency_codes`.
- **Invariant — tax-document numbering.** Numbers via `get_next_number(scope)` (FOR UPDATE), tracked in `number_sequences`, audited (in principle) in `number_sequences_audit`; proformas draw a separate 'proforma_invoices' series and must NOT consume tax-invoice numbers (EU VAT Art. 226 / GCC rationale in code); tax invoices have partial UNIQUE(tenant, invoice_number).
- **Multicurrency Decisions A/B/C: DONE, with residue.** A: functional-currency model (mandatory base per tenant, both amounts on the row) live via M1–M5 + PR #119. B: `numeric(19,4)` widening done with verified residue — `payment_allocations`, `receipt_allocations`, `payment_receipts` still carry NO currency/rate/base columns (an open allocation-level FX seam). C: snapshotted shared USD-pivot rates with a daily cron and carry-forward gap policy; manual overrides row-local.
- **The central seam a tax architecture must compose with.** `currencyService.resolveRateContext(docCurrency, onDate, override?)` is THE single rate-resolution seam; `financialMath` provides currency-aware rounding, base totals, and realized FX; `get_base_currency()` deliberately resolves to NULL (never USD) when unresolvable. New financial writes MUST populate `exchange_rate` + `*_base` through these seams.
- **Realized FX done; unrealized pending.** Payments carry their own frozen rate + base; the delta vs the invoice's booked rate posts an fx_gain/fx_loss row at allocation inside `record_payment`. Payments inherit the invoice's currency (SMB model); free cross-currency payments = Phase 2; unrealized/period-end revaluation = Phase 3, not started.
- **Multicurrency frontend done; explicit pendings.** Per-document currency selectors, '≈ base' previews, currency settings with base protection all shipped (+ the currency-p4 sweep). PENDING per architecture §4/§8/§11: the base-currency immutability lock trigger once financial rows exist, and the rate-staleness monitor. Phase 2 (multi-currency bank accounts + inter-account FX transfers) not started.
- **A Country Engine / Localization program is ALREADY underway — align, don't duplicate.** Phases 0–3 landed (config bags + no-stub guard; geo_regions/subdivisions/residency regions; `legal_entities` "tax identity ≠ tenant"; business units; FX shadow columns; geo_languages en+ar; portal preferred_language; notification overlays; governed override RPCs). Specs: `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-15-country-engine-design.md` + plans. This design builds on it.
- **Source freshness caveat.** `docs/data-recovery-workflow.md` reflects fixes through 2026-06-10; the multicurrency memory is dated 2026-05-30; the migrations manifest and generated types show later work. Treat the workflow doc's gap catalog as directionally canonical but re-verify individual financial claims against the manifest/live DB during implementation.

### B.2 Domain gaps adjacent to this program (not owned by it, but constrained by it)

- **Cross-border device shipping / customs:** custody transfers model INTERNAL handoffs only; no courier tracking, customs declarations, or cross-border evidence-shipping workflow for physical media that may bear personal data.
- **NDAs / consent per jurisdiction:** a fully-designed `ndas` table plus customer id fields have ZERO frontend usage — jurisdiction-specific NDA/consent variants cannot be built on a workflow that doesn't exist; fix the base gap first or jointly.
- **E-signature validity by jurisdiction:** no digital signature is stored anywhere (checkout prints a physical line; transfer signatures JSON-packed into notes; report approval unenforced) — eIDAS-style attribution requirements are unmeetable today.
- **Certificate of Destruction standards:** no structured CoD (media serials, method enum, sanitization standard, operator+witness signatures) — both a domain and a localization gap.
- **Tax on cross-border recovery services:** the tenant-level TaxConfig has no place-of-supply/export zero-rating/reverse-charge per document — exactly what the Part 2 tax engine adds, attached to `legal_entities`.
- **Customer portal legal attribution:** the portal is a sessionStorage pseudo-login with no Supabase JWT; approvals have no cryptographically attributable actor — weak against consent evidentiary standards any localization program layers onto it.
- **Language coverage beyond en/ar:** Arabic/RTL is genuinely supported in PDFs, but the ~2000-string t() sweep and the RTL layout sweep are deferred, and legal-document localization beyond en/ar is undesigned.
- **Allocation-level FX/precision residue:** `payment_allocations`/`receipt_allocations`/`payment_receipts` carry no currency columns; receipt settlement of foreign invoices is deliberately rejected — a constraint to respect or extend, not silently break.
- **Base-currency lock + rate-staleness monitor still pending:** statutory filing in base currency depends on both; do not silently assume them.
- **Financial audit/retention per jurisdiction:** `financial_audit_logs` has no live write path; per-jurisdiction filing periods, retention rules, and a genuinely written financial audit trail are needed as the return machinery lands.

**Judge rationale (condensed).** SPK wins on the three criteria the rubric declares existential (correctness, migration, forensics: 27/30 vs 25.5 and 24.5). Its core argument is the rubric's own: Indian GST correctness is a legal claim that must be provable, and typed TS strategies with externally-validated golden fixtures gated in CI are provable in a way predicate-jsonb rows are not. Config-First concedes in its own risks section that hard-regime logic escapes its closed DSL into "engine capabilities" (code), and that a bad published pack silently mis-taxing a country is "existential to the approach"; it also carries the largest migration delta (authoritative computation relocated into new PL/pgSQL plus a permanent TS-mirror parity burden) and the worst delivery risk (DSL interpreter + Studio + manifest all on the critical path). The Hybrid is the best pure fit and has the sharpest migration hygiene, but is weakest on forensics (pack-version-level provenance only — disqualifying-adjacent on a chain-of-custody platform) and its service-layer requirement gating leaves a REST-bypassable compliance hole. SPK's honest weakness — complex regimes need a code release (c2 6.5 vs 9.5) — is acceptable because the rubric demotes ideology below correctness, the six launch regimes are engineering-built phases under ALL three proposals anyway, and SPK's shared defaults keep ~80% of the world data-only.

## Appendix D — Alternatives considered

**Country Packs as Data — the Interpreter Architecture (Config-First).** One set of generic, country-blind engines (tax composer, return compiler, document-rule interpreter, numbering renderer, bank-file emitter) interpreting declarative, effective-dated, versioned rows — a country pack is nothing but data, authored in a Studio, validated by machine-run golden tests, published under dual control; new country = governed INSERTs, no deploy. It is the most intellectually complete proposal, has the best pure forensics story (per-row rule_ref + pack version means any figure is reproducible from data alone), and best serves the stated business goal of analyst-cost country onboarding. It was not chosen because it loses on the two criteria the evaluation declared existential: correctness — its own risks section concedes that India composition/RCM and US product taxability exceed the closed DSL and fall back to code, so hard-regime correctness converges on the plugin approach anyway, with the added hazard that legal logic is reviewed as predicate jsonb no type system or reviewer can check; and migration — it relocates the entire authoritative computation into a new PL/pgSQL engine AND flips the issuance path in one program (the largest behavioral delta of the three, touching the v1.2.0 custody invariants), while adding a permanent TS-mirror parity burden that is itself a wrong-money-on-screen vector. Its delivery risk was also the worst: a closed-DSL interpreter, a Studio product, a capability manifest, and dual-control publishing all sit on the critical path before country #2 ships. Its governance ideas were too good to lose — seven of the twelve grafts come from it.

**Compliance Kernel + Country Packs — the Hybrid Layered architecture.** Four jurisdiction-agnostic code primitives (multi-component tax engine, money kernel, effective-dated rule resolver, document field-requirement engine) fed by versioned data packs, with exactly four narrow plugin seams (e-invoice transports, payroll formulas, bank-file emitters, sub-national rate providers) and a crisply argued data/code line ("if a competent non-engineer could author it in a form and CI can validate it with a golden invoice, it is data"). It is the best pure fit with the repo's own roadmap and contributed the smartest single idea in the panel — split-by-place-of-supply and jurisdiction-stack as data-selected scheme modes of ONE engine — plus the sharpest migration hygiene (NOT VALID→VALIDATE, standing NULL monitors, publish-resync pinning). It was not chosen because it scored weakest on the highest-weighted forensics criterion: provenance is pack-version-level only, with no per-row rule trace and no plugin version/regime snapshot — the weakest "prove exactly what produced this figure" story on a platform whose brand is chain of custody — and because its issuance-time requirement gating ran in the service layer where a raw REST writer can skip it (its triggers enforce arithmetic and ledger posting, but not compliance gating). Five of the twelve grafts — including its kernel-primitive insight, which caps SPK's long-run per-country code cost — were absorbed into the final design.

## Appendix E — Open questions for the owner

1. **External tax review budget and sourcing.** Phases 4 and 5 carry explicit line items for an Indian CA and a US SALT reviewer to validate golden-fixture expected values (engineering cannot self-certify statutory math). Who engages them, at what budget, and is content-provider licensing (e.g., a rates/rules feed) an acceptable fallback for the long tail?
2. **Jurisdiction-watching staffing.** The Studio gives packs `next_review_date` and a staleness dashboard, but someone must own watching ZATCA waves, GSTR changes, and rate votes. Risk 11 names business staffing as a precondition to scaling past the six launch countries — who is the named owner, and when is the role staffed?
3. **US street-level accuracy posture.** The native stack is honest at subdivision granularity; product-level taxability (e.g., TX's data-processing rule) routes through an Avalara/TaxJar provider seam with per-tenant cost. Is provider licensing acceptable, and at which tenant tier does it become mandatory versus optional?
4. **Platform subscription billing scope.** Gap 3 shows the platform's own billing is broken and tax-blind (a distinct workstream from tenant-side tax). Does the owner want it folded into this program's Phase 3–5 window, or run as a separate track reusing the same currency/tax primitives? The multicurrency program doc currently excludes it by omission.
5. **Fiscalization-regime market decision.** Portugal/France/Germany/Italy-class certified-software regimes are honestly gated OUT by the capability manifest until the `certified_software`/`chained_document` regime classes are implemented. Are those markets in the 24-month plan (affects when the Phase-1 interface freeze gets exercised), or do they stay un-onboardable with a 422?
6. **Residency commitments.** The design enforces the 'global-1'-only invariant and the §2.8 residency 422. If a regulated deal (KSA PDPL localization, EU residency contract) lands, standing up a second Supabase region via `master_data_residency_regions.supabase_ref` is a significant infra project — is there a trigger threshold (deal size/market) at which it gets pre-built?
7. **Privacy-regime program ownership.** Gap 1's erasure/retention/DSR rebuild is scheduled in Phase 6 on the regime-key pattern, but two of its findings are broken-today (the erasure RPC, the export leaking `portal_password_hash`). Should those two ship in Phase 0 as hotfixes ahead of the regime parameterization?
8. **Unclaimed-device / abandoned-property law (flagged by the critic, beyond the top-6 gaps).** Every jurisdiction regulates what a lab may do with an uncollected or unpaid-for device: minimum holding periods, prescribed disposal notices, storage-fee accrual, and lien rights (UK repairer's lien vs US state abandoned-property statutes vs GCC rules). `custody_status` already includes 'disposed'/'archived' and checkout flows exist, but there is no retention clock, notice workflow, storage-fee concept, or per-country legality gate before a disposal event — a lab following the app's flow could destroy a device unlawfully. Where to look: the custody enums and `log_case_checkout`, `case_follow_ups` (no disposal-notice type), workflow-doc stages 13/15, and `geo_countries` (no retention/lien columns). Should this become a Phase-6 pack dimension (retention clocks + notice templates as country data) or a separate domain program?
9. **Statutory audit-file export formats (also flagged by the critic).** France's FEC (mandatory-on-demand 18-field journal file, penalties for non-production), Portugal's monthly SAF-T PT, Norway/Poland SAF-T dialects, and Germany's GoBD data-access mandate are precise per-country FILE-FORMAT obligations the country engine must eventually model as export descriptors — and the shipped `data_migration` engine (schema-driven Excel round-trips) is the obvious hook that currently has no compliance-format concept. Without naming this, the chart-of-accounts fix will land without the artifact layer that makes it legally usable in the EU. Where to look: `src/lib/dataMigration/workbookContract.ts`, `financialReportsService.ts` + `financial_transactions`, the 3-number `vat_returns` shape, and the registry (no reporting/export-format keys). Should export descriptors be added to the pack schema now (reserved, unimplemented) or deferred to the first EU country pack?
10. **Data-only country intake policy.** Once the Studio ships (Phase 3), simple-VAT countries become data-only publishes. Does the owner want an explicit market-driven intake queue (publish only where a prospect exists) or proactive coverage of, say, the full GCC + Commonwealth VAT set?
